§ about 01 / 08

A Nigeria-trained microbiologist who learned to read genomes in Mexico — and now studies foodborne parasites in Maryland.

I'm a Doctor of Science in Biotechnology (Instituto Politécnico Nacional, Mexico, 2019), with an M.Sc. in Pharmaceutical Microbiology from the University of Ibadan and a B.Tech in Microbiology from LAUTECH. My work has moved fluidly between wet-lab — culturing, PCR, SDS-PAGE, lipid profiling, Kirby-Bauer susceptibility — and dry-lab: whole-genome sequencing, assembly with SPAdes & Velvet, annotation with Prokka, and comparative genomics.

Right now I'm an ORISE postdoctoral fellow with Dr. Jenny Maloney at the USDA Agricultural Research Service in Beltsville, MD, isolating and sequencing foodborne parasites of animal and human origin. I also teach — a habit I picked up running junior-high biology classes in Ibadan long before I ever touched a FASTQ file.

This site is both a portfolio and a notebook. The teaching section holds step-through tutorials I use with the students I mentor. The AI tutor in the bottom-right corner is trained on my topics; ask it anything.

§ research 02 / 08

Three active threads.

Each thread has its own organisms, methods, and open questions — but they share a spine: use genome-scale data to explain what a bacterium is actually doing in its environment.

thread A // parasites

Foodborne parasites — isolation, genome assembly & bioinformatics

At USDA-ARS with Dr. Jenny Maloney: isolating and characterizing foodborne pathogens of animal and human origin, sequencing their genomes, and performing comparative analysis to map emerging risks.

USDA-ARS2024 – 2026ORISE fellowship
thread B // lipids

Sphingolipids & virulence in Caulobacter crescentus

Tested the role of sphingolipid biosynthesis in virulence using Galleria mellonella larvae injected with wild-type and serine-palmitoyl-transferase-deficient strains. Also hunting sphingolipid homologs in E. coli BL21(DE3) via SynTax and NCBI BLAST.

UNAM / CCGGeiger lab2021 – 2023
thread C // stenotrophomonas

Stenotrophomonas & the genomic basis of PAH degradation

Isolated multiple Stenotrophomonas from Mexican soil & sewage, characterized PAH-degrading capacity (GC & FTIR), profiled antibiotic susceptibility, and resolved four MDR genomes — ongoing comparative analysis of resistance & catabolic gene clusters.

IPN / CBGGuo lab2015 – ongoing

§ tools & datasets 05 / 08

Software and data I use — and occasionally contribute to.

SPAdes
Short-read assembler. Default for bacterial isolates < 10 Mb.
Velvet
Kept around for comparison & legacy projects.
Prokka
Rapid prokaryotic annotation; used on every Stenotrophomonas genome in our PeerJ & MRA papers.
BLAST+ / NCBI
For homology hunts — including finding sphingolipid biosynthesis homologs in E. coli.
SynTax
Synteny & taxonomy context for candidate gene clusters.
MEGA X
Multiple sequence alignment & phylogenetics.
FastQC / Trimmomatic
Read QC & trimming — step 1 of every assembly tutorial.
R & Python
Used at a working beginner's level for plotting & simple genome parsing.

Public data deposits

S. maltophilia SVIA2
Complete genome, Tabasco MX crude-oil soil. GenBank / MRA 2019.
Stenotrophomonas sp. Pemsol
PAH-degrader. PeerJ 2020. Genome + degradation assay data.
Bdellovibrio reynosensis
Novel species description. IJSEM 2022.
Sphingobium yanoikuyae S72
Complete genome. Applied Sciences 2022.
