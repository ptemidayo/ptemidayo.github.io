01 / Statement

A life between the gel, the field, and the terminal.

I'm a microbiologist trained at Obafemi Awolowo and in the Mexican CINVESTAV system. My work threads through three questions: what organisms live in places we overlook, what they can do for us (or to us), and how do we read that from their genomes. Today I run next-generation sequencing and assembly pipelines at USDA-ARS to detect parasites that cost livestock producers hundreds of millions a year. I also teach — because a pipeline unused is a pipeline forgotten.

02 / Selected work

Three threads.
Many organisms.

The cleanest way to understand my portfolio is through three ongoing projects. Each has grown into a distinct body of published work, but all three share a method: sequence first, ask questions once the data disagrees with you.

CASE 01USDA-ARS · 2024 —

Reading parasites before they read the herd

High-throughput sequencing pipelines to detect and identify livestock parasites at single-read resolution — so that outbreaks are caught weeks earlier than morphology alone allows.
CASE 02Geiger lab · 2024

The genes that make a virulent lipid

Identifying the bacterial gene set required to synthesise sphingolipids that provoke virulence — with Otto Geiger's group (bioRxiv, 2024).
CASE 03PeerJ · 2020

A Stenotrophomonas that eats oil

Discovery, genome analysis and PAH-degradation profile of Stenotrophomonas sp. Pemsol, isolated from contaminated Mexican soil.
CASE 04Braz. J. Microbiol. · 2021

Undoing the dye, bacterially

Two Stenotrophomonas strains from a Tepetitla textile effluent that decolorize and degrade azo dyes — an industrial-biotech application in progress.
CASE 05IJSEM · 2022

A predator in the soil

Naming Bdellovibrio reynosensis sp. nov. — a small predatory bacterium that hunts other Gram-negatives. Part of an ongoing exploration of bacterial predation as biocontrol.
